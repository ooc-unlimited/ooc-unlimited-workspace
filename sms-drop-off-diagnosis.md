## Why Leads Don't Respond

### 1. Generic First Message
If the first text reads like a mass blast, it dies on arrival. Licensed agents get dozens of recruiting messages. They can smell automation.

### 2. No Hook
"Are you interested in a new opportunity?" is not a hook. It's a delete trigger. There's no curiosity, no value proposition, no reason to type back.

### 3. Too Much Too Soon
Sending a wall of text about GFI comp structure in message #1 is the SMS equivalent of proposing on the first date.

### 4. No Follow-Up Cadence
One text â†’ silence â†’ move on. That's not a system, that's a hope strategy. Most sales happen between the 5th and 12th contact.

### 5. Wrong Channel for the Ask
SMS is for starting conversations, not closing deals. The goal of text #1 is to get text #2, not to get an ICA.

## The Fix: 7-Day SMS Sequence

### Design Principles
- **First text = curiosity, not pitch**
- **Each text has ONE job: get a response**
- **Personalize with their name and state**
- **Mix value, social proof, and direct asks**
- **Always end with a question (questions get replies)**

## A/B Testing Framework

### What to Test
1. **Opener style:** Curiosity vs. Value-first vs. Direct
2. **Timing:** Morning (9-11 AM) vs. Afternoon (2-4 PM) vs. Evening (6-8 PM)
3. **Follow-up cadence:** 1-3-5-7 days vs. 1-2-3-5-7 days
4. **Message length:** 1-2 sentences vs. 3-4 sentences
5. **Call-to-action:** Question vs. Statement vs. Offer

### How to Measure
For every 50 leads imported:
- **Response rate:** % that reply to any message (target: 15-25%)
- **Conversation rate:** % that engage in 2+ message exchange (target: 10-15%)
- **Call rate:** % that get on a phone/Zoom call (target: 5-10%)
- **ICA rate:** % that sign ICA (target: 2-5%)
