## ðŸªœ Micro-Commitment Ladders: The Antidote to Dropout

This is the single most important concept in modern onboarding psychology, and it's where your system has the biggest opportunity.

A micro-commitment ladder is exactly what it sounds like: a sequence of tiny, achievable actions that gradually increase in difficulty and investment. Each completed step creates a small dopamine hit and â€” critically â€” increases the psychological cost of quitting.

Here's what the best IMOs are doing in 2025-2026:

**Week 1: Identity Anchoring**
- Complete your agent profile (photo, bio, personal "why" statement)
- Watch one 15-minute training video and answer 3 questions
- Text your upline your #1 goal for the first 30 days
- Set up your tech tools (CRM login, calendar link)

**Week 2: First Contact**
- Shadow a live call or Zoom presentation
- Role-play one objection handling scenario
- Make your first 5 outbound calls (with a script provided)
- Report back: what surprised you?

**Week 3-4: Early Win Engineering**
- Close your first appointment (even if it's a friend or family member)
- Complete your first full product presentation
- Get your first "no" and debrief it with your mentor

**Month 2-3: Habit Lock-In**
- Weekly accountability check-in (5 minutes, structured)
- Hit your first commission or referral bonus
- Attend 4 consecutive team meetings

The magic is in the *sequence*. Each step is small enough to feel easy but meaningful enough to create momentum. Centric Consulting, which works with major IMOs, documented that automation-driven onboarding ladders with personalized drip campaigns measurably reduced turnover and shortened ramp-up time for new agents.
